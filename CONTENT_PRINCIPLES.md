# Content Principles for VICA Website

## Positioning

VICA is a **VR-native race-awareness instrument** for focused driver information. The site should reinforce that identity with precision, restraint, and care.

## Core communication rule

The website should communicate that the value is the **right race-critical data in the right place at the right time**, not more data.

## Voice

- Serious and practical.
- Respectful of the racing context and driver attention.
- Clear and concise, with low cognitive overhead.
- Conservative about certainty; avoid broad claims.

## Trust and safety posture

- Surface safety and control (especially disable/stop paths) early.
- Avoid implying guarantees about safety outcomes or elimination of all risk.
- Be explicit that users should validate setup in controlled contexts.
- Keep technical claims tied to published, public-safe behavior.

## What not to claim (before approval)

- No claims of broad simulator/platform compatibility.
- No claims of official approvals, sanctions, race licensing, or homologation.
- No claims this is an official race-control or anti-crash product.
- No claim of production-grade sales/checkout/account capabilities.

## Content guardrails

- Prefer plain words over hype.
- Avoid legal promises beyond defensible, conservative wording.
- Do not include private strategy, internal assumptions, pricing tactics, customer data, or internal support stories.
- Keep release-related communication honest and measured.
- Every page should make it easier to understand install safety and support options.

## Static-first expectation

Choose static pages, lightweight styling, and simple data files where useful. Favor readability, speed, and maintainability over feature complexity.
